Sanshir≈ç's mind was on "Great Dark Void," so he set the copy of Literary Review, still open to the page, next to his notebook. Furthermore, he was thoroughly absorbed in his own lecture and took no notice of Sanshir≈ç's imprudence. Sanshir≈ç enjoyed himself, note taking here and reading there. Doing the work of two men single-handedly, however, turned out to be an impossible feat. In the end, his lecture notes and "Great Dark Void" became jumbled in his mind. "How many years does nature take to form a jewel? And then how many years does it sparkle in silence before fortune discovers it?" This was the line he remembered. The rest was a blur. On the other hand, he'd made it through this whole period without once writing 'stray sheep' in his notes.
