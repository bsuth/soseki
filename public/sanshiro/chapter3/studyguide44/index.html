Rough translation

Sanshir≈ç wanted to answer, but he found himself unable to speak. In the meantime, the dark figure proceeded up the tracks. Following after, Sanshir≈ç realized he must be the owner of the other house that was up the lane from Nonomiya's. About fifty meters further on, the lanterns had stopped. The men were stopped too. They stood in silence with their lights held high. Without a word, Sanshir≈ç looked on. In the light of the lanterns was half of a body. The train had torn it from the right shoulder, under the breasts, to just above the waist, leaving behind an obliquely severed torso. The face was undamaged. It was a young woman.

Sanshir≈ç never forgot the sensations of that moment. He wanted to move away at once, and he turned his heels to do so, but his knees were so weak he could barely move. When he finally climbed the embankment and was back in the living room, his pulse was still throbbing. He called to the maidservant for some water, and was relieved to see she knew nothing of the matter. After a while, some commotion was heard from the house down the lane. Sanshir≈ç knew that its owner must have returned. Next, the area below the embankment buzzed with activity, after which all fell silent again. It was an almost unbearable silence.

The face of the dead woman appeared vividly before Sanshir≈ç's eyes. That face, and the words she'd uttered so despondently, hinted at a bitter fate that she'd left behind. It occurred to Sanshir≈ç that a life, the anchor of physical existence, could unravel in an instant, shed its illusion of resilience, and float away into the void. A sense of terror grabbed hold of his thoughts. It had all happened in the roar of a single instant. Until that instant, she had certainly been alive.
