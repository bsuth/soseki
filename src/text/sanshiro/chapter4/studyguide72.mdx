<h3 class="section">Rough translation</h3>
<p>The young lady hesitated for a moment. She was holding a large basket in her hand. As usual, the pattern of her kimono was unfamiliar. He was struck by its subtlety, which seemed in keeping with her style. The fabric had a dimpled sort of texture, over which ran lines in some sort of design. The design was wholly irregular.</p>
<p>A leaf would fall from time to time from the cherry branches above. One settled on the lid of her basket. No sooner had it settled than it was blown away. A gust of wind enclasped the young lady. She stood there, immersed in autumn.</p>
<p>"And you are ..." She spoke to Sanshirō as the gust moved on to the neighboring yard.</p>
<p>"I was asked to come and clean." He realized his reply was somewhat silly, as she'd seen him, in fact, sitting vacantly on the veranda.</p>
<p>She smiled and said, "Then perhaps I can wait with you for a bit."</p>
<p>She spoke as though seeking his consent, and this pleased Sanshirō greatly. "Yes," he answered. What he'd meant to convey was, "Yes, please wait with me." But the young lady remained standing, so Sanshirō followed with, "And you are ..." inquiring of her in the exact same way she'd inquired of him. At this she placed her basked on the veranda, took out a calling card from the pocket of her sash, and presented it to him.</p>
<p>On the card was 'Mineko Satomi.' Her address was the Masago section of Hongō, so she must live just across the way on the next hillside. As Sanshirō was studying her card, she took a seat on the veranda.</p>
<p>"We've met before." Sanshirō looked up at her after placing the card in his sleeve pocket.</p>
<p>"Yes, once at the hospital ..." She turned toward him as she spoke.</p>
<p>"And?"</p>
<p>"And by the edge of the pond." She answered immediately. She remembered it readily.</p>
<p>Sanshirō had run out of things to say. The young lady finally closed with a perfunctory, "Forgive me if I was untoward."</p>
<p>"No, not at all."</p>
<p>It was a markedly simple conversation. The two of them looked up at the cherry tree branches. On the ends hung a few last bug-eaten leaves. The moving party was late to arrive with its wares.</p>
