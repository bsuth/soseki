<h3 class="section">Rough translation</h3>
<p>"Professor, please let Sasaki have the upstairs room." Yojirō appealed on his own behalf.</p>
<p>Nonomiya laughed and replied, "I'll think of something. -- She looks grown up but she's really a foolish child. Quite a handful. And she insists that I should take her to see the chrysanthemum dolls at Dangozaka."</p>
<p>"Why don't you take her? I'd love to see them too."</p>
<p>"You'll come with us, then?"</p>
<p>"Absolutely. Ogawa-san, you should come too."</p>
<p>"I'd be happy to."</p>
<p>"And Sasaki-san."</p>
<p>"I'll pass on the chrysanthemum dolls. I'd rather go see a moving picture."</p>
<p>"Go and see the chrysanthemum dolls." Professor Hirota joined the conversation. "There's nothing like them when it comes to handiwork, even outside of Japan. They're an unparalleled example of human ingenuity that shouldn't be missed. If they'd used actual people in their display, not a single visitor would go and see. There are four or five actual people in every house, so there'd be no point in venturing so far as Dangozaka."</p>
<p>"Impeccable Hirota logic." Yojirō rated the professor's remark.</p>
<p>"It worked on us every time in my student days," Nonomiya commented.</p>
<p>"Come with us then," Mineko finally added.</p>
<p>The professor was suddenly speechless. His silence was followed by a round of laughter.</p> 
<p>"Need a little help here," the maidservant called from the kitchen.</p>
<p>"Coming," Yojirō shouted back as he jumped to his feet.</p>
<p>Sanshirō remained seated.</p>
<p>"Well, I guess I should be going." Nonomiya stood up.</p>
<p>"Do you have to go already? You just got here," Mineko replied.</p>
<p>Professor Hirota asked in parting if Nonomiya could wait a bit longer on the business they'd discussed. Nonomiya agreed and headed out through the garden. As soon as he'd disappeared through the gate, Mineko called out as though suddenly remembering something, hurried into the sandals she'd left at the edge of the garden, and followed after him. They talked out in the street.</p>
<p>Sanshirō quietly remained seated.</p>
