<h3 class="section">Rough translation</h3>
<p>"The professor said that Tōkyō is filthy and Japanese are ugly. Has he traveled abroad?"</p>
<p>"What do you think? That's the type he is. He gets that way because in his mind he's extrapolated everything far beyond reality. To his credit, he does study the Occident. He has lots of photographs; things like the Arc de Triomphe in Paris and places like the Houses of Parliament in London. It gets me how he compares Japan to these photographs and judges it filthy. And the real puzzler is that he's utterly indifferent to the untidy condition of his own house."</p>
<p>"He travels in 3rd class."</p>
<p>"Was he griping about the filth?"</p>
<p>"No, he never complained."</p>
<p>"Anyway, the professor's a philosopher."</p>
<p>"Does he teach philosophy at the school?"</p>
<p>"No, he only teaches English at the school. He's the type who gravitates toward philosophy as a matter of course. That's what makes him interesting."</p>
<p>"Has he published anything?"</p>
<p>"Nothing. He writes an essay on occasion, but to no effect. He's getting nowhere. The world is oblivious to his ideas. He compared me to a little round lantern, but the professor himself is a great dark void."</p>
<p>"Sounds like he needs to engage more with society."</p>
<p>"Speaking of engagement -- The professor never takes initiative in anything. If it weren't for me he couldn't manage three meals a day."</p>
<p>Sanshirō laughed incredulously.</p>
<p>"I kid you not. His lack of initiative borders on pathetic. It always falls on me to direct the maidservant 'til everything's handled to the professor's satisfaction. -- But trivial matters aside, I'm preparing a major campaign to get him instated at the university as a full professor."</p>
<p>Yojirō was serious. Sanshirō was surprised by his bluster. Indifferent to Sanshirō's reaction, Yojirō went on. "When we move, will you come and help us?" He spoke as though he already had a place lined up.</p>
