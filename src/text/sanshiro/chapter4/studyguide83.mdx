<h3 class="section">Rough translation</h3>
<p>Sanshirō couldn't help but notice Nonomiya's demeanor and the direction of his gaze.</p>
<p>Mineko stood up and went to the kitchen. She washed a cup, poured fresh tea, and brought it out to the edge of the veranda. "Have some tea," she said, taking a seat there herself. "How is Yoshiko doing?"</p>
<p>"She's pretty well recovered." Nonomiya sat back down and took a sip of tea. Then he turned toward the professor. "Professor, after moving all the way to Ōkubo, it looks like I may have to come back."</p>
<p>"Why is that?"</p>
<p>"My sister doesn't like walking past the Toyama Academy training fields on her way to school and back. And she says she's lonely waiting up for me while I run my experiments in the evenings. It's okay for now with my mother there, but she'll be going back to the country soon, and then it will be just my sister and the maidservant. Two faint-hearted women can't manage on their own. -- It puts me in a bind." Half for show, he let out a long sigh. Then he turned to Mineko. "Satomi-san, any chance you could take in a house guest at your place?"</p>
<p>"I'm sure we'd be happy to."</p>
<p>"Who's the guest? Sōhachi or Yoshiko?" Yojirō interjected himself.</p>
<p>"Either is welcome."</p>
<p>Only Sanshirō remained silent. Professor Hirota asked, in a more serious tone, "Then what will you do?"</p>
<p>"Once my sister's taken care of I can lodge somewhere for a while. Otherwise I'll have to move all over again. I'd like to put her in the school dormitory, but she's really still a child. We need an arrangement where I can go to her or she can come to me at any time."</p>
<p>"Then Satomi's place is your only option." Yojirō offered his verdict.</p>
<p>The professor paid no heed to Yojirō. "She could stay here in the upstairs room, but I've got Sasaki to deal with as well."</p>
