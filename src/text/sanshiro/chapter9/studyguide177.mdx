<h3 class="section">Rough translation</h3>
<p>Sanshirō, without answering, began taking notes. He'd written several lines when Yojirō leaned close and added, "When I've had money, I've often lent to others. Not a one has paid me back. That's the key to my sunny disposition."</p>
<p>Sanshirō found this hard to swallow. He smiled lightly and continued with his notes. Yojirō left it at this and was silent through the rest of the lecture.</p>
<p>The bell sounded, and the two of them left the room together. Yojirō asked abruptly, "Do you think she's in love with you?"</p>
<p>Other students from the lecture were filing past. Sanshirō descended the stairs in silence, turned out the side entrance, and emerged onto the open field by the library. Only then did he turn back to Yojirō. "I'm not sure."</p>
<p>Yojirō studied him for a moment. "Maybe not. But suppose you were sure. Do you think you could be her husband?"</p>
<p>Sanshirō had never considered this question. He'd assumed Mineko's affection to be the sole and sufficient qualification. Now that the question was raised, he wasn't so sure. He tilted his head in thought.</p>
<p>"Nonomiya could be," stated Yojirō.</p>
<p>"Is there a connection between them?" Sanshirō's expression was hard and serious.</p>
<p>"Who knows?" replied Yojirō lightly. "Anyway, go see Nonomiya and get your lecture." With that he headed off toward the pond.</p>
<p>Sanshirō stood rooted in place, a blank expression etched on his face.</p>
<p>Yojirō stopped himself after five or six paces, then circled back with a grin. "Hey, how bout taking Yoshiko instead?" So saying, he pulled Sanshirō along toward the pond. As they walked, he repeated his suggestion. By and by, the bell sounded again.</p>
