<h3 class="section">Rough translation</h3>
<p>It was night this time, and the roof was too dark to see, but an electric light burned in the room. At first sight of the electric lighting, Sanshirō, to his amusement, recalled the thatchwork.</p> 
<p>"A curious pair of guests. I take it you met outside?" Nonomiya addressed his sister.</p>
<p>She corrected his supposition, and went on to suggest that he buy a shirt like Sanshirō's. The she told him her Japanese-made violin, recently purchased, was poorly crafted. She'd been patient in waiting, so the least he could do was exchange it for something nicer, something at least on par with Mineko's. After that came a prolonged venting of similar such grievances. Nonomiya did not get cross with her, but he also offered no sympathy. He merely nodded in acknowledgment.</p>
<p>Sanshirō stood by silently. Yoshiko's issues were all of a trifling nature. Once she let loose, she didn't hold back. Yet he didn't regard her as foolish, and he couldn't judge her as selfish. This engagement with her brother, as he stood and listened, felt refreshing, like an outing through a sunlit meadow. He even forgot the impending lecture. Suddenly, in this moment, she caught him off guard.</p>
<p>"Oh, I almost forgot. Mineko gave me a message."</p>
<p>"She did, huh?"</p>
<p>"You don't have to hide your joy. You are glad, aren't you?"</p>
<p>Nonomiya looked bashful. He turned to Sanshirō. "See what a fool my sister is?"</p>
<p>All Sanshirō could do was grin.</p>
<p>"I'm not a fool in the least, am I?"</p>
<p>Sanshirō grinned again. Deep down, he was tired of grinning.</p>
<p>"There's a performance at the Literary Society. Mineko requests your company."</p>
<p>"Why doesn't her brother take her?"</p>
<p>"He said he had a prior engagement."</p>
<p>"And you're going too?"</p>
<p>"Of course."</p>
