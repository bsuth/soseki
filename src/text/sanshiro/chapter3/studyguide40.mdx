<h3 class="section">Rough translation</h3>
<p>"Did you come all this way just on that account?"</p>
<p>"Well, no. Not really."</p>
<p>"Actually, your mother sent me a nice gift from the country for helping you to get oriented here. I just wanted to say a word of thanks to you as well."</p>
<p>"Ah, I see. She sent you something?"</p>
<p>"Yes, it's a red fish pickled in saké lees."</p>
<p>"Must be himeichi."</p>
<p>Sanshirō thought this was a poor gift. However, Nonomiya was eager to know more about it. Sanshirō gave detailed information, especially on how to eat it. He explained that it should be broiled in the lees, but when the time came to serve it, the lees should be discarded. Otherwise they would rob the fish of its flavor.</p>
<p>As the two of them were discussing himeichi, it grew dark outside. Sanshirō thought he should be going and was preparing to take his leave when a telegram arrived. Nonomiya broke the seal and read it. Then he muttered, "Darn," to himself.</p>
<p>Sanshirō couldn't ignore Nonomiya's reaction, but he was also reluctant to pry into the other's business. He asked dryly, "Has something happened?"</p>
<p>"No, nothing serious." Nonomiya held out the paper for him to see. It said 'come at once.'</p>
<p>"You have to go somewhere?"</p>
<p>"My younger sister is ill. She's in the hospital at the university, and she wants me to come at once." So saying, there was not the least hint of urgency in his voice. Sanshirō was the more flustered of the two. Nonomiya had a younger sister, who was ill, who was in the university hospital. These new facts, jumbled with thoughts of the young lady he'd encountered by the pond, put his mind in a whirl.</p>
<p>"It sounds serious."</p>
<p>"It's not so serious. My mother's there with her. -- If this were serious then she would have come right away to fetch me by train. -- This is just my sister pestering me. The foolish girl pulls this all the time. I haven't gone to see her since my move, and because it's Sunday she expected me today. That's why." As he spoke, Nonomiya tilted his head to the side and considered what to do.</p>
<p>"You should probably go. Just in case."</p>
<p>"I suppose. Her condition can't have changed much in just four or five days, but I guess I should go see her."</p>
<p>"It's probably best that you do."</p>
