<h3 class="section">Rough translation</h3>
<p>"You look a bit pale."</p>
<p>"Do I?"</p>
<p>They walked on in silence for five or six paces. Sanshirō wanted to tear away the thin barrier that seemed to hold them apart. His judgment failed him, however, when it came to choosing the right words. Words of indulgence, like those from a novel, were out of the question. They didn't suite his taste, and they weren't befitting of a young pair acquainted just socially. In all honesty, Sanshirō was wishing for the impossible. And he didn't stop at wishing. As they walked, he was racking his brain.</p>
<p>"Did you have some business with Haraguchi today?" Mineko spoke first.</p>
<p>"No, nothing of any importance."</p>
<p>"Then it was just a social call."</p>
<p>"No, it wasn't a social call."</p> 
<p>"Oh. What was it then?"</p>
<p>Sanshirō seized the moment. "I went to see you." Sanshirō felt that, with this, he had said all there was to say.</p>
<p>Mineko was utterly unperturbed. Further, she responded back in her usual beguiling tone. "I couldn't accept the money there."</p>
<p>Sanshirō was beside himself. They walked for a while in silence. Then he suddenly spoke. "The truth is, it wasn't about the money."</p>
<p>Mineko didn't answer for a moment. Then she said quietly, "I don't care about the money either. You keep it."</p>
