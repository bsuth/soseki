<h3 class="section">Rough translation</h3>
<p>In the afternoon of the first Saturday after opening, the whole gang arrived en masse -- Professor Hirota, Nonomiya, Yojirō, and Sanshirō. Leaving other works for later, the four of them headed straight to the "Lady and Woodland" gallery. "There it is, there it is!" Yojirō exclaimed. A large crowd filled the room. Sanshirō hesitated at the entrance. Nonomiya proceeded with full composure.</p>
<p>Sanshirō glimpsed the work from in back of the crowd and turned away. He leaned against the bench to wait for the others.</p>
<p>"Look at the size of it," Yojirō marveled.</p>
<p>"They say he was hoping to sell it to you," the professor replied.</p>
<p>"Not me. It was ..." Yojirō, seeing Sanshirō at the bench with a vexed expression, held his tongue.</p>
<p>"The man has a flair with color. It's really quite chic." Nonomiya offered his assessment.</p>
<p>"It's almost too sharp. He did confess that he could never paint the sound of the tsuzumi," the professor added.</p>
<p>"What does that mean, to paint the sound of the tsuzumi?"</p>
<p>"It means to paint to a simple rhythm, sophistication be damned."</p>
<p>The two of them laughed. They were focused on the artist's technique, but Yojirō took a different view. "Who could paint Miss Satomi otherwise? Sophistication is a given."</p>
<p>Nonomiya put his hand in his pocket, searching for a pencil to mark his program. He didn't find a pencil, but he did draw forth a printed card. It was his invitation to Mineko's wedding party. The party was now long past. Nonomiya and Professor Hirota had attended, dressed in their frock coats. Sanshirō, on the day of his return to Tōkyō, had found his invite on the desk in his room, the date already passed.</p>
<p>Nonomiya tore up the card and discarded it onto the floor. After a bit, he and the professor moved on to other works. Yojirō remained and came to Sanshirō's side.</p>
<p>"What did you think of 'Lady and Woodland?'"</p>
<p>"I don't like the title."</p>
<p>"What should he have called it?"</p>
<p>Sanshirō gave no answer. The words in his mind, though, were 'Stray Sheep.'</p>
