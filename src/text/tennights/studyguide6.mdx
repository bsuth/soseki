<h2 class="section">Rough translation</h2>
<p>3rd Night</p>
<p>This is what I saw in my dream.</p>
<p>I'm carrying a six-year-old child on my back. I believe it is my own child. At some point I'm aware that, curiously, the child is blind and its head is shaved. I ask how long it's been blind, and it says for a long time. The voice is clearly that of a child, but the words are those of an adult. It shows me no deference in its speech.</p>
<p>On either side of us are green fields of new rice. Our path is narrow. Occasionally, a snowy heron flashes in the twilight.</p>
<p>"We're in the fields, aren't we," I hear from behind.</p>
<p>"How can you tell?" I turn my head and ask.</p>
<p>"Don't you hear the herons' cries?" comes the answer.</p>  
<p>There follow several cries of a heron.</p>
<p>Though it's my own child, I feel afraid. As long as I carry this burden, I imagine myself in peril. I look ahead, wondering if there isn't some place I can abandon it, and I see a deep, dark forest. I decide that that's the place. In the same moment, I hear a quiet laugh from behind.</p>
<p>"Why do you laugh?"</p>
<p>The child doesn't answer, but instead asks, "Father, am I heavy?"</p>
<p>"You're not heavy," I reply.</p>
<p>"I soon will be," it answers.</p>
