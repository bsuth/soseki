<h3 class="section">Rough translation</h3>
<p>5</p>
<p>I entered to the left through a nursery that fronted the cemetery, traversing a lane with maples planted down both sides. From a tea house at the end of the lane, a man resembling Sensei emerged. I moved nearer, close enough to see the sun reflect off the rims of his glasses. Without further hesitation, I called out, "Sensei," in a loud voice. Sensei stopped in his tracks and turned my way.</p>
<p>"How did ... ? How did ... ?"</p>
<p>Sensei repeated these words twice over. Their tone, as he repeated them, rung heavy in the midday calm. I found myself at a loss for an answer.</p>
<p>"Did you follow me here? How did ...?" Sensei's demeanor was perfectly relaxed, and his voice was perfectly restrained. However, a shadow of some sort seemed to pass over his countenance.</p>
<p>I explained to him how I'd come to be there.</p>
<p>"Did my wife tell you whose grave I came to visit?"</p>
<p>"No, that she didn't say."</p>
<p>"I see. -- I expect she wouldn't, having only just met you. There's no reason she should."</p>
<p>Sensei seemed finally assured. The meaning of this all eluded me.</p>
<p>On our way back out to the boulevard, Sensei and I passed among the grave sites. There were graves marked 'Isabella so-and-so' and 'Login, Servant of the Lord.' Nearby stood a grave post inscribed with 'All Living Creatures are Endowed with the Essence of Buddha.' Another grave said 'Minister Plenipotentiary so-and-so.' I stopped in front of a small post engraved with 'An · Toku · Retsu' and asked Sensei about the reading of the characters. "They intended, I suppose, that we read those as 'André,'" he replied with a wry smile.</p>
