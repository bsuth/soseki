<h3 class="section">Rough translation</h3>
<p>A strange unease washed over me. I tried to read but couldn't concentrate. An hour passed, and Sensei appeared below my window and called my name. Surprised, I opened the window. He asked from below if I'd walk with him. I took out my watch, which was still tucked into the folds of my sash, and saw that it was a bit past eight. I was still dressed as for my visit, so I immediately went out front.</p>
<p>Sensei and I drank beer together that night. Sensei was not a heavy drinker. If the first few drinks didn't cheer him, he wasn't one to double down by drinking more.</p>
<p>"It's no use tonight," he said with a forced smile.</p>
<p>"The drink's not helping you?" I asked with some sympathy.</p>
<p>For my part, the earlier incident had been weighing on my mind the entire time, making me anxious. It was akin to a fishbone stuck in one's throat. I would think about speaking to Sensei frankly, then reconsider and decide not to. These inner vacillations, no doubt, presented themselves outwardly as an odd sort of restlessness.</p>
<p>"You seem out of sorts tonight," Sensei broached the subject. "The truth is, I'm not myself either. I suppose you've noticed."</p>
<p>I had no answer to give.</p>
<p>"I quarreled with my wife earlier. I let petty emotions get the better of me."</p>
<p>"Why did you ..." I couldn't bring myself to voice the word quarrel.</p>
<p>"My wife misunderstands me. And when I try to tell her so, she refuses to listen. I got cross with her."</p>
<p>"How is it she misunderstands you?"</p>
<p>Sensei did not respond directly to my question. "If I were the kind of man she thinks I am, then I'd have no need to suffer so."</p>
<p>The nature and extent of Sensei's suffering were entirely unknown to me.</p>
