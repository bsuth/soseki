<h3 class="section">Rough translation</h3>
<p>As I talked with Sensei's wife, our discussion, in due course, fell first to Sensei and then to this very subject.</p>
<p>"Why is it that Sensei is so studious in his own home yet not engaged with the world outside?"</p>
<p>"Engagement is out of the question. He won't even think of it."</p>
<p>"Because he views it as futile?"</p>
<p>"I can't say how he views the world -- as a woman it's not my prerogative to know. However, I don't believe that's the case. I think he wants to engage. He wants to, but he can't. It's terribly unfortunate."</p>
<p>"But he seems quite capable. He's perfectly healthy, is he not?"</p>
<p>"His health is fine. There's nothing wrong with him."</p>
<p>"Then what could stop him from pursuing an occupation?"</p>
<p>"I'm afraid I can't answer. If I knew that much, then I wouldn't worry so. I'm in the dark, unable to ease his plight."</p>
<p>In her voice was great sympathy. Still, she managed to put on a faint smile. On the surface at least, I was the more intent one, brooding in silence. As if suddenly remembering, she spoke again.</p>
<p>"He wasn't always like this. He was a different man in his youth. He's changed completely."</p>
<p>"How long ago do you mean?"</p>
<p>"His student days."</p>
<p>"You knew Sensei in his student days?"</p>
<p>She blushed a bit.</p>
