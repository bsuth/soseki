## Rough translation

Given this shared history between us, the words "Those not seeking betterment of the spirit are nothing but simpletons" were no doubt a slap in K's face. As I've stated before, though, it was not my intent to tear down that which he'd worked so hard to build up. On the contrary, I wanted him to build it higher. Whether this led him to "the way," or whether it led him to nirvana did not concern me. I simply feared that any abupt swing in his worldview could bring his interests into conflict with mine. In short, my words were purely self-serving.

"Those not seeking betterment of the spirit are nothing but simpletons."

I repeated these same words again. Then I watched to see their effect.

"A simpleton," he finally answered. "That's all that I am."

K came to a stop where he was. His eyes were cast downward. A chill ran through me. I feared I'd pushed him too far and braced myself for a backlash. However, I also noted the lack of any life in his voice. I wanted to read his eyes, but he didn't turn my way. He slowly resumed walking.
